'Kill a Pain' or 'Make a Gain'


This is my motto for developing software or making games. I learned this phrase while learning marketing fundamentals at University: the needs of a market is only met if the product does this. How can I make life easier for myself or others? What can they gain from playing my game? Fun? Skill? Competition? Satisfaction?


I started from humble beginnings. Pixel colour detection to automatically reset my Pokemon emulator to get a rare Shiny. Bypassing school laptop security and creating and administrator account. Making an "Impossible Game" for my High School Softare Dev project. From learning object-oriented programming with Java and Unity to building Mobile web apps with React Native.


Sometime in second year, I thought about Minecraft being made from Java, and my own skills at Java, so I put two and two together: Applying my skills to making minecraft mods. Parsing the chat log output to automatically answer trivia questions. A gameshow that heals you for correct answers and strikes you with lightning for incorrect ones. Then in Third Year: a full games project from scratch .


I love programming. Problem solving. Finding a way to use my skills to get ahead.
